Research Interests & Works

Traffic Safety Analysis

Focuses on developing analytical, simulation-based, and data-driven models to represent traffic flow dynamics and network behavior. Emphasis is placed on optimizing traffic performance through advanced algorithms, congestion mitigation strategies, and system-level efficiency improvements.

Application of LiDAR in Transportation Systems

Explores the use of LiDAR data for high-resolution mapping, roadway geometry extraction, traffic monitoring, and infrastructure assessment. This research leverages point cloud processing and sensor fusion to enhance transportation planning, operations, and safety analysis.

Traffic Operations and Control

Concentrates on the analysis and optimization of traffic control strategies, including signal timing, adaptive signal control, and corridor-level operations. This work aims to improve mobility, reduce delays, and enhance system reliability under varying demand conditions.

Surrogate Safety Measures

Investigates non-crash-based safety indicators such as time-to-collision, post-encroachment time, and conflict severity. These measures enable proactive safety assessment using trajectory data, simulation outputs, and video analytics before crashes occur.

Big Data Analytics in Transportation

Applies machine learning, statistical modeling, and large-scale data processing to transportation datasets such as probe vehicle data, sensor data, and connected vehicle information. The objective is to extract actionable insights for traffic prediction, system optimization, and decision support.

Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS)

Focuses on the integration of sensing, communication, and control technologies to improve transportation efficiency and safety. Research includes connected and autonomous vehicles, real-time traffic management, and smart infrastructure applications.

Transportation Planning

Focuses on the systematic analysis of travel demand, land-use interactions, and multimodal network performance to support long-term transportation decision-making. Emphasis is placed on accessibility, equity, sustainability, and data-driven planning approaches for developing efficient and resilient transportation systems.

Smart Cities

Explores the application of digital technologies, real-time data, and intelligent infrastructure to enhance urban mobility and city operations. This research integrates transportation systems with IoT, big data analytics, and connected technologies to improve efficiency, safety, and overall urban livability.

Publications

Signal Optimization to Improve Traffic Conditions: A Case Study of Satdobato and Gwarko Intersections, Kathmandu Valley | 2024
Nepal Journal of Civil Engineering
Link: Click Here to View Publication
Abstract: This study introduces a methodology for assessing intersection performance and examining varioussignalization strategies. The methodology was applied to analyze and optimize traffic signals at twointersections in Kathmandu Valley, namely Satdobato and Gwarko. Micro-simulation software ’SIDRAIntersection 9.0’ was utilized to model these intersections, with calibration and validation performed usingfield data. The performance assessment primarily focused on average delay and back of queue length,followed by network evaluation. The current operational performance at Satdobato showed an average delayof 226.5 seconds per vehicle, while Gwarko had an average delay of 201.1 seconds per vehicle. Sensitivityanalysis identified the basic saturation flow as the most critical parameter for both intersections. Optimizedsignal phasing, the leading right-turn configuration with optimal timing with a cycle time of 100 seconds forSatdobato, and the existing scenario with optimal timing with a cycle time of 70 seconds for Gwarko resultedin reduced average delays of 51.8 and 52.5 seconds per vehicle, respectively. Network analysis for thecoordinated intersection led to a back-of-queue length of 606 m, slightly higher than the 578.9 m observedfor the optimized individual intersections. Despite substantial improvements with optimized signals, signalcoordination appeared to be ineffective in this particular case.
